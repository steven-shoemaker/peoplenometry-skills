Charts

Three archetypes cover every exhibit a people-analytics brief needs. Complete inline SVG — every bar, point, label, and value drawn in the markup itself, so the chart is fully correct with JavaScript off, in print, and the instant the file opens. A tiny inline script (the REPORT JS block at the bottom of the report) then layers on hover tooltips and a one-time entrance animation. Zero external requests — no CDN, no chart library, no webfonts, ever. Copy these patterns verbatim and swap the data.

  • Zero external requests. No CDN, no chart library, no webfonts. Reports get forwarded, opened offline, printed, and read in locked-down mail clients.
  • Static SVG first, JS second. The markup alone is the finished chart. JS adds tooltips (data-tip / data-tip-title) and entrance animation (class="chart-anim" + data-anim="grow|rise") — enhancement only; no layout depends on it.
  • One accent per chart. --chart-accent marks the finding; everything else is --chart-neutral.
  • Bars start at zero. Always. No exceptions, no truncated axes.
  • Label directly. Values at bar ends, annotations at the point of interest. No legends, ever.
  • Every chart shows its n in the figcaption.
  • Never meaning by color alone. The accented element also carries a text annotation, so grayscale printing survives. Accessible: role="img" + a descriptive aria-label on every SVG.

People analytics brief

Why are people leaving?

Trailing 12 months (Jul 2025 – Jul 2026) · Regular employees · Prepared 5 Jul 2026 · For: CHRO

Executive answer

Company-wide attrition is healthy at 13.2% — but that hides the story. Voluntary attrition among mid-tenure sales reps runs 23.6%, nearly 2.4× the company rate, concentrated among reps paid below band: 74% of those leavers sat under band midpoint, and 19 of the 31 were rated top performers. Not a culture problem — a compensation-and-progression gap in one function, costing you trained revenue.

Finding 1 · The concentration

One cohort — sales ICs at 12–24 months — leaves at 2.4× the company rate

Voluntary attrition across the company is 9.8%, and every department sits within a normal band. The outlier is not a department at all: it's a cohort. Sales individual contributors in their second year leave at 23.6% — a cliff that department-level reporting averages away.

Finding 2 · Why

The leavers were underpaid: 74% sat below band midpoint

Within the cohort, pay separates who left from who stayed. 74% of voluntary leavers were paid below their salary band's midpoint (compa-ratio < 0.95), against 31% of stayers. The medians tell the same story: leavers sat at a compa-ratio of 0.88; stayers at 1.02. These reps could see their own pipeline — and their own paycheck — and the market resolved the gap for you.

Finding 3 · What it's costing you

You are losing your best reps, not your weakest

Regrettable attrition — trained reps at peak productivity · n=31

This is not performance-managed churn. Nearly two-thirds of the cohort's leavers carried a 4 or 5 rating — ramped reps walking out at exactly the point their quota capacity peaks, taking the training investment and the pipeline with them.

Recommended next moves
  1. Market-adjust the below-band mid-tenure sales ICs now. Targeted, not a company-wide program — model the adjustment against the fully-loaded cost of replacing a ramped rep (6–9 months of lost quota).
  2. Add an 18-month comp-and-progression checkpoint to the sales IC track. The cliff sits at 12–24 months — before anything currently intervenes.
Appendix · Methodology & caveats

Definitions

Voluntary attrition
Voluntary leavers ÷ average headcount over the same window, annualized.
Cohort
Sales function, individual-contributor levels, 12–24 months tenure at exit (or as of the window end, for stayers).
Compa-ratio / below band
Salary ÷ salary-band midpoint. Below band = compa-ratio < 0.95.
Source
Peoplenometry metrics library v1.0.
Population
Regular employees only. Contractors, interns, and temporary workers are excluded.

The numbers

  • Window: 1 Jul 2025 – 1 Jul 2026 (trailing 12 months). Company average headcount: 912.
  • Leavers in window: 120 total, 89 voluntary → 13.2% total / 9.8% voluntary attrition.
  • Cohort (Sales · IC · 12–24 mo): average headcount 131, 31 voluntary leavers → 23.6% annualized.
  • Compensation: leavers median compa-ratio 0.88 vs. stayers 1.02; 74% of leavers below band midpoint.
  • Performance: 19 of the 31 cohort leavers rated 4–5 in their most recent cycle.

Caveats

  • The pay–attrition relationship is an association, not proof of cause; exit-interview data would confirm it.
  • Single 12-month window — no prior-year baseline, so the trend direction is unknown.
  • Manager-level detail is withheld to avoid identifying individuals.

To strengthen this analysis

  • Code exit-interview reasons for the cohort's leavers and reconcile against the pay finding.
  • Add a prior-year comparison to establish whether the cliff is new or chronic.
  • Benchmark the sales IC bands against current market comp — 0.88 vs. midpoint may understate the gap vs. market.
